What I Do

Product Design

User Research. Brainstorming. Planning, design, and testing. A product can be born without these, but its success hinges on its evolution throughout the iterative design process. Teams working together to ideate, design, and implement like a well-oiled machine turn visions into working products that can change the world.

Documentation

A good design can wow even the pickiest clients. However, without documentation, it is difficult to walk someone through your iterative process. Whether it is providing detailed notations for a user or producing resources for internal use, documentation will always be an imperative part of product design.

Prototyping

Tangibile components bring life to static designs. Binding strong visuals to interactive elements transforms even the most basic designs into visual masterpieces. Prototyping allows us to resurrect an idea without engineering the final product, making it a crucial cog in the wheel of iterative design.

Management

"The only thing worse than a bad decision is continuing with a bad decision." Sometimes even the best of us make mistakes or get stuck, but with good guidance anyone can succeed. If there is one aspect of work I take the most pride in, it is managing an energetic team that is willing to respect their peers and work hard to deliver meaningful products.
